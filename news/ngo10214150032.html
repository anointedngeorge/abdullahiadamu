Governor Adamu meets Non-Governmental Agencies

Governor Abdullahi Adamu has held a meeting with all non-governmental agencies operating in Nasarawa state. The object of the meeting was to challenge the NGO�s on the need to join government in the fight against all forms of societal ills and the upliftment of the economic status of the rural populace.

Addressing the meeting, Governor Adamu stated that NGO�s must see themselves as veritable partners in the development of the state. He urged them to ensure that they conducted their activities in such a way as to fulfill their objectives. Governor Adamu frowned at the practice whereby NGO�s fought to be given subventions by the state. He reiterated that as independent agencies, they were duty bound to conduct their activities without any intervention from the government. He therefore urged them to source for fund from various donor agencies.

Speaking earlier, the newly appointed Special Adviser on Cooperatives and Non-governmental agencies, Mrs. Sophie Embu said that over one hundred NGO�s have been registered in the state and they were currently being trained on the ways of attracting funds from donor organizations. She said appreciative awareness has been generated through her office and that they will all be galvanized for greater impact on the socio-economic plane.
