Military must strive to defend democracy

Governor Abdullahi Adamu has called on the Nigerian military to support the democratic process in Nigeria. Governor Adamu made this call while receiving the General Officer Commanding, 3rd Armoured Division, Jos at a courtesy call.

The Commanding officer who is on a tour of units under his commanded visited Governor Adamu in the office as part of the schedules for the tour. Governor Adamu used the occasion to commend the cordial relationship existing between the state government and the division. He said Nasarawa state would always identify with the aspirations of the division.

Governor Adamu commended the men of the Division for their role in restoring peace to the southern senatorial district which was enmeshed in crisis late last year. Aside from this, the men have also curtailed the activities of men of the underworld and bandits as well as exhibited a high sense of discipline. He therefore implored them to continue in their peace-keeping efforts.

Earlier the GOC, Major-General Joseph Owonubi intimated the Governor on the fact of his tour. He said it was essential for him to go round all the units of the command to familiarize himself with the forces. He said having been posted to the division just two weeks ago, it was necessary to complement the oral brief he had received with a physical assessment of the facilities at the disposal of his men. He thanked the government and people of Nasarawa state for the support given to the division and emphasized that peace and stability were an essential factor of a thriving democracy. He therefore pledged the loyalty of the men of the division to the present democratic administration.
